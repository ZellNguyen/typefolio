The Rookie - Public VR Theatre

Jan 2017 - May 2017

The Rookie was an industry project in the second term of the Master of Digital Media Program. Our mission is to expand the boundaries of VR by creating a public narrative experience in a physically immersive environment.

My Role

Lead programmer, Technical artist:
In charge of creating the modules for other programmers, managing the source control to resolve conflict and dependencies, managing art assets integration, reviewing and optimizing code before merging, and coding some important modules in Unity3D.

My accomplishments

  • Optimized code and graphic performance to increase the fps from 40 to 80 by using caches, loop optimization, async threads, and graphic optimization (such as occlusion culling, etc.)
  • Built the VR object-interaction feature by integrating VR trackers, Leap Motion and Arduino into Unity3D (C#).
  • Fixed the calibration issue of VR by writing a script to constantly track and update VR headset's status.
  • Reduced the number of people feeling nauseous in VR by 70% by experimenting some VR nausea-reducing techniques.
  • Made a presentation at CVR 2017 - Canada's biggest VR/AR Conference.

Challenges

  • It was the first time I worked with VR.
  • VR object-interaction was a fairly new topic so there wasn't any official documentations to learn from.
  • The HTC Vive's calibration is not accurate and stable because it's not designed for object interaction.
  • With limited resources, we had to prototype with whatever we had at hand.
